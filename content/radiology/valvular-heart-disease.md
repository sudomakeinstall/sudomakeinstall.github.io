## Normal Pressures (mmHg)

- RA: 0-4
- RV: 25/4
- PA: 25/10
- LA: 8-10
- LV: 120/10
- Ao: 120/80

## Bernoulli Equation

Driving pressure + Kinetic energy + Potential Energy = Constant (along a streamline)

p/d + v²/2 + gh = Constant

Where:
- p: Static pressure (Pa)
- d: Density (1060 kg/m³ for blood)
- v: Velocity (m/s)
- g: Acceleration due to gravity (m/s²)
- h: Elevation (m)

### Simplified Bernoulli Equation

1. p₁/d + v₁²/2 + gh₁ = p₂/d + v₂²/2 + gh₂ → Negate elevation
2. p₁/d + v₁²/2 = p₂/d + v₂²/2 → Solve for Δp
3. Δp = d/2 × (v₂² - v₁²) → For blood, d = 1060 kg/m³
4. Δp = 530 kg/m³ × (v₂² - v₁²) → Convert Δp from Pascal to mmHg (1 mmHg = 133.322 Pa)
5. Δp = 530 kg/m³ × 1 mmHg/133.322 Pa × (v₂² - v₁²) → Simplify, noting 1 Pa = kg/(m×s²)
6. Δp = 4 mmHg × (s/m)² × (v₂² - v₁²) → If v₁ << v₂, v₁ can be neglected
7. **Δp = 4v²** (Δp in mmHg, v in m/s)

## Continuity Equation (Velocity Time Integral)

Cross sectional area × Velocity = Constant (at any point)

- A₁ × V₁ = A₂ × V₂ → Solve for V₂
- V₂ = V₁ × (A₁/A₂) → Used as indirect measurement of valve area in aortic stenosis

## Regurgitation Equations

\* indicates preferred method [1]

- EDV - ESV = SV
- LVSV = MVRF + AVFF = MVFF + AVRF
- RVSV = TVRF + PVFF = TVFF + PVRF

### MR (Mitral Regurgitation)
- **\*MR = LVSV - AVFF**
- MR = LVSV - PVFF
- MR = LVSV - RVSV

### AR (Aortic Regurgitation)
- **\*Direct diastolic reverse volume at aortic root**
- AR = AVFF - PVFF
- AR = LVSV - RVSV

### TR (Tricuspid Regurgitation)
- **\*TR = RVSV - PVFF**
- TR = RVSV - AVFF
- TR = RVSV - LVSV

### PR (Pulmonic Regurgitation)
- **\*Direct diastolic reverse volume at PA**
- PR = RVSV - LVSV
- PR = RVSV - AVFF

## Tricuspid

### Anatomy
- Anterior, septal, and inferior/posterior leaflets
- AV node sits at the base of the septal leaflet
- Primary cordae attach to the free edge of the leaflet
- Secondary cordae attach midway down the leaflet
- Highly variable structure; proposed nomenclature [2]

### Stenosis (echo criteria)
- Mean pressure gradient: ≥5 mmHg
- Inflow time-velocity integral: >60 cm
- T1/2: ≥190 ms
- Valve area by continuity equation: ≤1 cm²

### Regurgitation

\*Can be seen on CT; others are echo-based [3]

**Mild-moderate:**
- \*Regurgitant volume <45 mL/beat
- \*Effective regurgitant orifice <0.4 cm²
- Central jet <50% RA
- Vena contracta width <0.7 cm

**Severe:**
- \*Regurgitant volume ≥45 mL/beat
- \*Hepatic vein systolic flow reversal
- \*Effective regurgitant orifice ≥0.4 cm²
- Central jet ≥50% RA
- Vena contracta width ≥0.7 cm
- Dense continuous wave signal with triangular shape

**Ancillary findings:**
- Annular dilatation >40 mm or >21 mm/m² (indexed to BSA)
- Tenting >8 mm
- TAPSE <1.6 cm

Per 2020 ACC/AHA guidelines, regurgitant fraction plays no role in defining mild/moderate/severe.

## Pulmonic

### Anatomy
- Anterior, right posterior, and left posterior leaflets

### Stenosis
| Severity | Peak Gradient | Peak Velocity |
|----------|---------------|---------------|
| Mild | <36 mmHg | <3 m/s |
| Moderate | 36-64 mmHg | 3-4 m/s |
| Severe | >64 mmHg (mean >35 mmHg) | >4 m/s |

### Regurgitation

**Indications for surgery:**
- Regurgitant fraction >40%
- RV:LV diameter or EDV ratio >2
- RVEDVi >150 mL/m²
- RVEF <45%
- Large RVOT aneurysm

| Severity | Volume | Fraction |
|----------|--------|----------|
| Mild | <30 mL/beat | <25% |
| Moderate | 30-40 mL/beat | 20-35% |
| Severe | >40 mL/beat | >35% |

## Mitral

### Anatomy
- Two leaflets, each with three scallops
- Numbered from anterolateral to posteromedial (A1-P1, A2-P2, A3-P3)
- Anterolateral and posteromedial papillary muscles
- Solitary PM thickening >1.1 cm or anteroapical displacement of the PM suggest HCM

### Stenosis

\*Can be seen on CT; others are echo- or cath-based [3]

| Severity | Valve Area | Diastolic Pressure Half-time |
|----------|------------|------------------------------|
| Mild-to-moderate | >1.5 cm² | <150 ms |
| Severe | <1.5 cm² | ≥150 ms |

Severe also includes PA systolic pressure >50 mmHg.

### Regurgitation

**Terminology:**
- **Normal:** Coaptation plane sits above annular plane
- **Flail:** Leaflet free edge flexes backwards into the LA (rupture/elongation of primary cordae)
- **Billowing:** Body of the leaflet extends into the LA (rupture/elongation of secondary cordae)
- **Prolapse:** Both body and free edge extend into the LA (rupture/elongation of primary and secondary cordae)
- **Functional:** Secondary to annular dilatation (cordae are fine)

**Mild ("At risk of MR"):**
- Central jet area <20%
- Vena contracta <0.3 cm

**Moderate ("Progressive MR"):**
- Central jet 20-40% or late systolic eccentric jet
- Vena contracta <0.7 cm
- Regurgitant volume <60 mL
- Regurgitant fraction <50%
- Effective regurgitant orifice <0.4 cm²
- Angiographic grade +1 or +2

**Severe:**
- Central jet >40% or holosystolic eccentric jet
- Vena contracta ≥0.7 cm
- Regurgitant volume ≥60 mL
- Regurgitant fraction ≥50%
- Effective regurgitant orifice ≥0.4 cm²
- Angiographic grade +3 or +4

**Secondary MR:**
- Annulus diameter >35 mm
- Annulus/anterior leaflet ratio >1.3 (on 3CH)

## Aortic

### Anatomy

No true annulus; several structures have been called the annulus:
- Sinotubular junction
- Ventriculoaortic junction (preferred by surgeons)
- Virtual base ring/nadir (preferred by interventional cardiologists)

**Sievers classification:**
- Named for number of raphes (Type 0, 1, 2)
- If no raphes, named for whether coronaries come off the same cusp ("AP") or different cusps ("Lat")
- If raphes present, they are indicated in the name (e.g. L-R for type 1, L-R/R-N for type 2, etc)

### Stenosis

Direct measurement of opening area, indirect by continuity equation (A_aorta = A_lvot × V_lvot / V_aorta), pressure gradient (5 mm above the valve).

| Severity | Area | Peak Pressure | Mean Pressure | Peak Velocity |
|----------|------|---------------|---------------|---------------|
| Normal | — | — | — | <2 m/s |
| Mild | 1.6-2.0 cm² | <36 mmHg | <20 mmHg | 2-3 m/s |
| Moderate | 1.0-1.5 cm² | 36-64 mmHg | 20-40 mmHg | 3-4 m/s |
| Severe | ≤1.0 cm² (or AVAi ≤0.6 cm²/m²) | >64 mmHg | >40 mmHg | >4 m/s |
| Very Severe | — | — | ≥60 mmHg | ≥5 m/s |

### Regurgitation

| Severity | Volume | Fraction | Orifice Area | Jet Width | Vena Contracta |
|----------|--------|----------|--------------|-----------|----------------|
| Mild | <30 mL | <30% | <0.10 cm² | <25% | <3 mm |
| Moderate | 30-59 mL | 30-49% | 0.10-0.29 cm² | 25-65% | 3-6 mm |
| Severe | ≥60 mL | ≥50% | ≥0.30 cm² | >65% | >6 mm |
